The E-Prescription App is the official app for e-prescriptions in Germany. gematik publishes the app as the National Agency for Digital Medicine on behalf of the legislator. gematik is also responsible for the app’s data protection.

About this privacy policy

(Status: March 2026)

In this privacy policy, we inform you about data processing by the E-Prescription App. We strive to present the technical processes in an understandable way. If we have not succeeded in doing so, please let us know. You can find our contact details under Contact Persons.
This privacy policy does not apply to the services of other providers involved in the e-prescription system. Information on the responsible providers of these services can be found under Other Responsible Parties.

What is this app used for?

If you receive an electronic prescription from your doctor, it is stored in encrypted form in the prescription service. The prescription service is the central prescription storage in the German health network. With the E-Prescription App, you can securely access the prescription service and receive, manage, and redeem e-prescriptions.

Is the use of the app voluntary?

Yes, the use of the E-Prescription App is voluntary. You are also not obliged to provide certain data in the app or to log in. However, if you do not log in, you cannot use all functions of the app.

How and for what purposes is your data processed?

Set up profiles

You must set up at least one “profile”.

  • The data for the profile is stored only on your device.

  • The profile name is used for clear display. It can be freely chosen.

  • You can set up multiple profiles to manage e-prescriptions for relatives.

Log in to the prescription service

Only the patient (or a representative), the prescribing doctor’s practice, and the dispensing pharmacy may access the data in the prescription service. For every access to your data in the prescription service, the identity must therefore be proven in order to check authorization. This identity verification is carried out by the respective responsible identity service. Information about the provider responsible for you can be found under Other Responsible Parties.

  • You can prove your identity using your electronic health card (eGK) or your HealthID:
    If you log in with the eGK, the digital authentication certificate on the chip is read contactlessly (via NFC) and transmitted to the identity service. You give your consent by entering the PIN. The authentication certificate contains your insured person master data (e.g., your name and insurance number) and is considered electronic proof of identity. Once the identity service has checked the authentication certificate and confirmed your identity, it issues the E-Prescription App with an access key (token). This allows the E-Prescription App to log in to the prescription service.

  • If you log in with your HealthID, you must first select your health insurance so that the E-Prescription App can connect to the “correct” app. The list of available insurance apps is retrieved by the E-Prescription App from the identity service. After you have shared your identity data via your insurance app with the E-Prescription App, it forwards the data to the identity service for verification. If your identity can be confirmed, the identity service issues the access key (token) for logging in to the prescription service. After logging in, your e-prescriptions and dispensing information stored in the prescription service are downloaded and additionally stored on your device.

If you manage your e-prescriptions via the E-Prescription App, your activities are transmitted to the prescription service and implemented there. These activities include, for example, granting access rights and deleting e-prescriptions.

Save access data

If you are logged in via the eGK+PIN method, you can activate the function “Save access data” for future logins. The authentication certificate and access key (token) are then stored as local access data in a protected area of your device so that they can be reused. This function can be activated if your smartphone has access protection, e.g., biometrics or system PIN.

Scan and redeem prescription codes

If you want to store a prescription code and redeem it at a pharmacy via the app, you must first scan it with the camera. When scanning, an electronic copy of the prescription code is created. This step takes place exclusively on your device, meaning no internet connection is required. You can then redeem the e-prescription via the app at a pharmacy:

  • If you are logged in to the prescription service and assign the e-prescription to a pharmacy for redemption via the app, this is documented in the prescription service and the prescription status is changed to “in redemption”. The pharmacy then receives access rights to the e-prescription stored in the prescription service. There is no direct communication between the app and the pharmacy. You can view the current processing status, which is managed by the prescription service, at any time in the app.

  • If you are not logged in to the prescription service, you can transmit the prescription code together with your additional order data (e.g., whether you want to pick up or have the medication delivered) directly to the pharmacy. In this case, the app communicates directly with the pharmacy’s communication system. The prescription service is not involved in this communication and is therefore not informed about the order. For your overview, the transmitted e-prescription is marked as “sent” in the order overview of your app. The app receives the access data for the pharmacy’s communication system from the pharmacy directory.

Pharmacy search

When using the pharmacy search, your search criteria (e.g., addresses or location data) are transmitted to the pharmacy directory. The pharmacy directory then provides the app with a list of pharmacies matching the search criteria.

Note for Android devices:

For the map view, the app uses the Google Maps service with your consent. The app then transmits your location (if you have enabled it) and the locations of the pharmacies found to an interface of your operating system (Google Maps interface). This interface is part of the Google Play Services already installed on your device. The use of Google Maps is subject to Google’s Terms of Use for Google Maps/Google Earth and the Google Privacy Policy.

Messages to pharmacies

The E-Prescription App enables direct encrypted and confidential communication between you and pharmacies.
If you are logged in to the prescription service, communication with the pharmacy runs via the prescription service, which also archives the messages. The app downloads the messages from the prescription service and stores them on your device so that you can see the current status even without logging in. Neither the provider of the prescription service nor gematik has access to the exchanged messages.
If you are not logged in, you can send messages directly to the pharmacy’s communication system via the app. The app receives the access data from the pharmacy directory. The prescription service is not involved. Please note: to receive messages and view message histories, you must log in to the prescription service.

Note for Android devices:

To reliably translate pharmacy messages, the app uses a special text processing interface from Google ML Kit. This interface is part of the Google Play Services already installed on your device. The processing of captured texts takes place exclusively on your device.
However, as with all Android devices with Google Play Services, Google reserves the right to log certain usage and device data generated when using the interface and to transmit them to Google servers for further development. It cannot be ruled out that these data are transferred to a third country (e.g., the USA).
The use of the translation function is voluntary. A corresponding language model is downloaded and used with your consent.

Digital cost receipts (for privately insured persons)

As a privately insured person, you can consent in the app to receive digital cost receipts. Your consent is recorded in the prescription service and can be withdrawn at any time in the app. If you consent, you will receive cost receipts from pharmacies only in digital form via the prescription service. You can manage and save them as electronically submitable PDF documents via the app. For easy submission, you can also share the PDF documents directly with your health insurance’s submission app.

Security functions

Connected devices

The “Save access data” function can be activated on multiple devices. Each device is then considered a “connected device”. All connected devices are centrally managed by the responsible identity service. For this purpose, the app transmits device information (manufacturer, model, OS version, device name) to the identity service, which stores it. You can view connected devices in the settings.
You can deregister a device anytime. The identity service then deletes the stored device data and access keys.

Integrity checks

Smartphones can run manipulated operating systems (“jailbreak” or “root”). Therefore, each time the app starts, a technical check is performed. If manipulation is detected, a warning is shown.
Apple devices: storage is checked for jailbreak indicators (e.g., Cydia).
Android/Huawei: system integrity APIs are used to evaluate device/app integrity.

App access lock

The app can be automatically locked after closing. Unlocking is required on reopening. Password or biometric methods are available.
If “Save access data” is activated, the app lock is automatically enabled.
Passwords are stored locally.
Biometric data is not stored—only the result is used.

Push notifications

The app can send reminders and updates (e.g., new Präskription). You must allow push notifications.
Medication reminders are processed locally.
For service updates, the app registers with the OS push service and receives a push identifier. Messages are sent encrypted via gematik’s push gateway.
Services used: Firebase (Google), Apple Push, Huawei Push. Data transfer to third countries cannot be excluded.
Messages contain no prescription content, only general notices.

To whom are your data disclosed?

gematik has no access to your stored data. Data is only shared as described.
Operating system providers may collect device and usage data and transfer them to third countries.

When are your data deleted?

gematik stores no personal data. You can delete local data in the app. Prescriptions are deleted after 100 days; receipts after 10 years. Logs are deleted after 3 years.
Deleting a profile deletes all related data. Uninstalling the app deletes all local data.

Your data protection rights

You have rights to access, rectification, deletion, restriction, and data portability (GDPR). You may object to processing. You may lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (BfDI).
